Hello! I'm Damilare Babatunde

And this is my portfolio. But first, allow me to walk you through my path to become a Data Analyst. I graduated from The Federal Polytechnic in Ilaro, Ogun State, with Higher National Diploma in building technology. My interest in data exploded after I enrolled in a course on data analysis and visualisation, thanks to the Nigerian Federal Ministry of Communication and Digital Economy's 3 Million Technical Talents (3MTT, as it is often known). As an advocate for peace of mind, I quickly realise the immense potential that exists in data analysis, visualisation, and interpretation to make perceptive judgements and execute well-informed activities that guarantee peace of mind for all parties involved in the business. With a background in the construction industry and a strong aptitude for learning, listening, critical thinking, and problem-solving—I enjoy coming up with solutions to challenging tasks—I am confident that my journey in the data world will be fruitful and a source of inspiration for my potential clients. I'm available for both short-term tasks and longer-term, part-time commitments.

Here's the what I do.

Using data cleaning, processing, and visualisation to assist daily business decisions that promote global change in order to project peace of mind.

Prepare

With a background in building technology and construction management, I possess both theoretical and practical expertise in effective evaluation and survey design. I've worked with google form, excel sheet, basic knowledge in SQL, and other data collecting and management tools.

Process

Owing to my expertise with Power Query and DAX, as a Power BI developer, I can provide assistance with enhancing current workflows and developing new ones that cover data transformation, unification, spatial and predictive analysis, and other topics.

Visualise

As a Power BI Developer, I have a deep understanding of best practices for data visualisation. I've made static infographics and interactive tools that are accessible online and in print.
